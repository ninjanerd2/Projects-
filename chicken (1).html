This is The Fried Chicken Recipe

Ingredients

  • Chicken: 1.8 kg (4 lbs) whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • Marinade: 1 cup buttermilk (or substitute with 1 cup whole milk mixed with 1 tablespoon lemon juice)
  • Dry Breading: 2 cups all-purpose flour, 1 cup cornstarch (for extra crispiness)
  • Seasonings: 1 tablespoon paprika, 1 tablespoon garlic powder, 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional), salt, and black pepper to taste
  • Frying Oil: 1.5 to 2 quarts of neutral oil with a high smoke point (e.g., vegetable, canola, or peanut oil)

Step-By-Step Instructions

  1. Marinate The Chicken
    Place your chicken pieces in a large bowl or zip-top bag and pour the buttermilk over them. Add a pinch of salt and pepper. Let this marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 to 4 hours, or overnight for the most tender, juicy meat.

  2. Prepare the Breading
    In a large, shallow dish or a paper bag, mix the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, and your seasonings. Stir well until evenly combined.

  3. Dredge the Chicken
    Remove one piece of chicken from the buttermilk, letting any excess drip off. Toss the chicken thoroughly into the flour mixture, pressing down firmly so the coating adheres completely. For an extra crispy and "craggy" texture, you can drizzle 1-2 tablespoons of the buttermilk marinade directly into the dry flour mixture and rub it to form small, crunchy lumps before coating the chicken.

  4. Let it rest
    Place the breaded chicken on a wire rack or baking sheet and let it rest for 5 to 10 minutes. This allows the flour to absorb the moisture and form a paste-like crust that will not easily fall off in the fryer.

  5. Fry to Perfection
    Pour your oil into a heavy-bottomed pot (like a cast-iron Dutch oven) until it is about 5 to 7 cm deep. Heat the oil over medium-high heat until it reaches 175°C (350°F) on an Instant-Read Thermometer. Carefully drop the chicken into the oil in small batches—do not overcrowd the pot, as this will drop the oil temperature and make the chicken greasy.
    • Dark Meat (thighs/drumsticks): Fry for about 12-15 minutes.
    • White Meat (breasts/wings): Fry for about 8-10 minutes.

  6. Drain and Serve
    Using tongs, carefully lift the chicken out when it reaches a deep, dark golden brown and has an internal temperature of 74°C (165°F). Transfer the chicken to a wire cooling rack placed over a baking sheet to drain the excess oil. Sprinkle with a little Kosher Salt immediately after frying, and let the chicken cool for 5 minutes before serving.
