This is a Spaghetti Bolognese Recipe

Ingredients

  • Meet: 500g ground beef (minced beef)
  • Veggies: 1 onion, 1 carrot, and 1-2 celery sticks (all finely chopped)
  • Aromatics: 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • Base: 2 tbsp olive oil, 2 tbsp tomato purée (paste)
  • Sauce: 2 cans (400g each) of chopped or plum tomatoes, 1 beef stock cube, 125ml dry red wine (optional)
  • Herbs & Seasoning: A pinch of dried oregano, salt, and black pepper
  • Pasta: 400g spaghetti
  • Topping: Grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Sauté the Aromatics
    Heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a large saucepan or heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, carrot, and celery. Cook for about 5 to 7 minutes until the vegetables are soft and translucent. Add the crushed garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  2. Step 2: Brown the Beef
    Turn the heat up to medium-high. Add the ground beef to the pot, along with a pinch of salt and pepper. Cook while stirring constantly, breaking up any large clumps with a wooden spoon until the meat is thoroughly browned.

  3. Step 3: Add the Sauce
    Pour in the red wine (if using) and let it bubble for 2 minutes to cook off the alcohol. Stir in the tomato purée and let it cook for a minute. Next, add the canned tomatoes, dried oregano, and crumble in the beef stock cube. Stir well and bring the mixture to a boil.

  4. Step 4: Simmer Slowly
    Once boiling, reduce the heat to the lowest setting and cover with a lid. Let it simmer gently for at least 45 minutes to 1 hour, stirring occasionally. The longer it simmers, the richer and more tender the sauce will become.

  5. Step 5: Cook the Pasta
    When the sauce is almost done, bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook according to the package instructions until it is al dente (firm to the bite). Drain the pasta, reserving about a cup of the starchy cooking water.

  6. Step 6: Combine and Serve
    Add the cooked spaghetti directly into the simmering bolognese sauce, tossing everything together so the pasta is perfectly coated. If the sauce is too thick, splash in a little of the reserved pasta water to loosen it. Serve hot with freshly grated Parmesan cheese.
